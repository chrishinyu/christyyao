SYNC


UI / UX Design


Overview

This entrepreneurial project presents SYNC, a crowdsourcing platform that allows users to collaboratively edit news articles, consolidating context and different standpoints about a news event and thereby synchronizing users’ understandings of it.


Role

UI / UX Designer

User Research, Design Guildline, Design System

Febuary 2020 - January 2021



SYNC

SYNC is a platform aimed at leveraging the crowd to coedit news articles. Its name was inspired by our motivation to create a platform that synchronizes news audiences’ knowledge and understanding of news events.



Background

Manipulation of truth is a critical problem of nowadays media, and without any doubt, users usually face misinformation and disinformation both on news websites and, especially, in social media.



It has been observed that news media in Taiwan are politically and ideologically fragmented and polarized, and that misinformation, rumors, and misleading news are ubiquitous there. Despite the laudable efforts of certain fact-checking organizations and web services, as well as volunteers, who strive to stem the tide of misinformation.

Design Process


Concept

The objective of developing SYNC, therefore, is to curate a more synchronized form of news, marked by clearer context and a wider array of viewpoints and sources, via a combination of crowdsourcing and AI.


Given that different people possess different levels and types of knowledge, and read news articles about the same event that are written from a wide range of perspectives, they should be able to collaboratively combine and consolidate such information in a unified, non-segregated manner and distribute it via a range of different platforms, feeds, and posts.




Structure

The current high-level architecture of SYNC consists of a web-service interface, a back-end server, a number of news crawlers, and backend databases.



Details

We ideated through sketches and came out with the main design. The news platform service we want to provide contains four main elements: news homepage, news editor, personal page, and reading page.

Lo-fi Prototype & Usability Testing

We iterated our design by conducting usability testing with the low-fidelity prototype. Our participants provided feedbacks on error-prevention methods, accessibility, and discoverability of the UI.



Design Iteration

We integrate and analyze the feedback from user testing, revise the design, improve the originally proposed functions and architecture, and propose a more user-friendly design experience.



Design System

After the iterative design phase, the results of the design are gradually converged, and design guidelines are proposed for the system.




User Flow

Propose the user flow of the main four pages. Plan the user flow for specific user goals to understand how many pages or steps the user has to go through to complete a specific task.
