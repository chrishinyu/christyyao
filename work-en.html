HOPE English
UX and Usability Evaluation


UX Design / UX Research


Overview

In this project, we cooperated with an English learning product - “HOPE English”. We systematically evaluate the usability and functionality of interactive systems using a variety of methods in order to assess needs and behavior of target users.


Role

Project Lead, UX Designer, Researcher

Interview, User Research, Comparative Analysis, Heuristic Evaluation, Survey, Usability Test

Febuary 2019 - July 2019



Background

"希平方 HOPE English" is an English learning platform, divided into web and mobile device version. The system will recommend courses based on the user’s English level. Each subjects includes learning new courses, sentence, listening and spelling and dictation tests etc. Through the planned learning method and customized courses, it cloud improve users' English listening, speaking, reading and writing skills.


Design Process


Details

Interaction Map

Present the core path of the user experience of the services and define the scope of the interactive map. In this way, we can better understand the main provided services and features, as well as the needs and goals of the users.


Use this document to start a discussion with the client-HOPE English and find out possible pain points in the experience process. In addition, from the information and background they provided, the scope of the problem was defined.


Define

We want to understand the needs and behaviors of users for this service, and then explore the reasons that cause users to interrupt or suspend use.



Contextual Inquiry and Affinity Diagramming

Conducting contextual inquiry with our target users to uncover any pain points that they were experiencing with the existing user journey. Besides, through the interviews we got to understand their needs, tasks, contexts, and goals. In this process, we interviewed with 12 users who have used "HOPE English" to learn English, investigating their usage habits, behaviors, and motivations. We also deeply explored the reasons why users temporarily or permanently interrupted the system.


After collecting the recordings from the user interviews, I conducted affinity diagram with my teammate to synthesise and gather insight. We grouped these problems under common themes and features in the platform.



Personas and Scenarios

While based on user research finding, we created three type of personas and scenarios for the product. It provide an easy way to describe the target user of the service, supporting user-centered design by making characteristics of key user segments more salient.




Comparative Analysis

Comparing the features and usability of HOPE English system with comparative systems. It provides strategic insights into the features, functions, flows, and feelings evoked by the design solutions of other services. By understanding these facets of competitors’ products, we could strategically propose our solution or design with the goal.



Survey

Using survey to investigate users opinions and experiences. We sent online questionnaires to users and received a total of 457 valid questionnaires, mainly to survey the reasons why customers continue or interrupt their use. Through quantitative analysis, understand the potential problems and shortcomings of the service, and summarize the corresponding improvement suggestions.




Heuristic Evaluation

We examine the interface and judge its compliance by established heuristics (Nielsen-Molich's). Using checklists to evaluate the system from several points of view and identify both major and minor problems with an interface.




Usability Test

Design a few tasks for participants who have never used HOPE English APP before. Before performing the task, the research team will interview the participants to understand their experience of online language learning platforms. After they finished the task, the research team will ask the participants to fill in the SUS scale, and observes the participant’s behavior and listens for feedback to find out more details. The usability test not only helped us discover new usability problems, but also verified the problems found in previous heuristic evaluation.
