LUEUR


Installation


Overview

LUEUR is an interactive installation, exploring the possibility of light materialization and provides new interpretation of light. We aim to provide tangible interaction with light and facilitate imagination to create a visuo-haptic illusion and challenge the theory of nature — light becomes tangible and receives gravity.


Role

Project Lead / Designer / Developer

Unity, Processing, Hardware Fabrication, Prototyping, Tangible interaction design

December 2019 - May 2020



Background

Light, as we interpret in general, is an element without gravity and free material that does not exist in tangible form. In terms of science, light can be perceived as waves or particles. Light has been one of the great sources of inspiration for artists, they create the light installation, light art which applied art form utilized natural or artificial lights as the main medium of expression has also enriched our understanding of light.


Design Process


Concept

The origin of the creation of this work is a fanciful and unrealistic concept. I want to reinterpret the ”light”, as we knew which is an element that exists without any form and weight, it has no weight. Then it brings me an inspiration to explore the tangible interaction with light and regard light as the main body of expression. Let the light behave like a physical object with weight, conduct different physical movements under the influence of gravity. Moreover, it could be perceived in different sensory outputs, while operating it with active motion inputs.



We propose LUEUR to explore the design space of tangible interaction with light and expands our imaginative horizons providing new perspectives of light. The experience is designed to make LUEUR transport participants’ imagination into a thought experiment, allowing them to make their own interpretations. They could directly touch, manipulate, and feel the light in the tangible interaction.



Details - Interaction system

The interactive system is the core of the interactivity in this work and the aesthetic experience.



We have built the haptic sensation simulation mechanism to convey different tactile feedback, while users interact with a visually represented object.
Users could perform various operations that represent different effects of light motions. With different C/D ratios, they interact with the installation to perceive pseudo-haptic feedback that may generate various haptic sensations of light.



We constructed a physical object in the virtual environment with Unity to simulate the movement of light affected by gravity. The physics engines on the platform can display objects that enable physical behavior such as the use of gravity, collision,etc physic interactions.



The design of visual effects system helps to increase the feedback of visual interaction, and visual feedback also plays a significant role in the haptic simulation system. The visual effects involve the integration of live-action and generated imagery (digital or optical effects) to create environments, inanimate objects which look realistic.



The light is vividly glowing in the device, with its illumination seems to be simulating a certain kind of biological, fluid effects or particle effects, etc. LUEUR is just like a device with an organism, made manifest through light, lives inside the sculpture.



Details - Hardware Fabrication

The design of the hardware system includes the process of ideation by creating sketches and image boards, and the coming process of prototyping iteration to the final version of the style and structure. Next is the approach of sensing and lighting control, which is the power supply of the entire work operation and motion tracking to support the interaction system.



Demostration

LUEUR would demonstrate as an interactive installation, providing the participants with a perceptual and immersive experience of light. The users could carry out such acts of direct touch, manipulate, in order to engage in the tangible interaction and perceive the pseudo-haptic feedback that simulates different haptic sensations.



Experiment Study

Through participant study and experimental design, I want to further verify whether the implementation of this system has achieved the desired outcomes and reached the designed effects as well as to optimize or evaluate the experience. The people who take part in the study to experience LUEUR are not just a viewer or user, however, they become active “imaginers”. Through the process of interacting with the installation transporting viewers’ imagination into a thought experiment allows enough room for them to make their own interpretations. The experience of the participants is also part of a work of art, and they engage in the creation of the work.
